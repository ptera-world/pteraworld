# Proposed Fragments

Atomic ideas that recur across multiple essays and would work as standalone graph nodes. Each fragment is a single insight, not an argument. The goal is to extract these into short (2-4 sentence) nodes that essays can link to instead of re-explaining.

## 1. the metric replaces the thing it measured

When you compress a goal into a number, people optimize the number and the goal disappears. Goodhart's law as a universal pattern: grades replace learning, engagement replaces satisfaction, follower counts replace connection, asset values replace housing.

## 30. emergence is the thing you can't predict from the scaffolding

Complex systems produce behavior their architects can't predict. You design the rules but not what emerges from them. This is what makes a system alive rather than a menu. It's also why you can't plan your way to the thing you want - you can only keep building and see what surprises you.
